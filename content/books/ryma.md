A masterpiece of world-building. Herbert layers ecology, religion, politics, and prophecy into something that feels less like a novel and more like an artifact from another civilization.

**Why I love it:** Paul Atreides isn't a hero in the usual sense — the book is sharper than that. It's about what people do with myths, and what myths do to people.

**Read it if you like:** dense prose, slow burns, desert worlds, court intrigue.
