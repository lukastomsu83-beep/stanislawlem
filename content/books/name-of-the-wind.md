The story of Kvothe — orphan, thief, prodigy, legend — told by himself, looking back. Lyrical, frustrating, and addictive in equal measure.

**Why I love it:** Rothfuss writes prose that sounds like music. The magic system feels earned, the music sequences are genuinely moving, and Kvothe is exactly as insufferable as a teenage genius would be.

**Read it if you like:** coming-of-age stories, magic schools done right, prose that takes its time.
